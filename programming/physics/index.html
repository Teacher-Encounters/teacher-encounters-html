Physics Simulation

In this two-part activity, you will be doing the following.

  • Writing a simple simulation to demonstrate how acceleration, velocity and position relate to each other.
  • Modifying an existing program

Each step will show you the code you need to add, and the expected output.

Step 1 - Access the Development Environment

p5.js
  • Press the play button
  • You should see a grey square, a background of 200 where greyscale goes from 0 -> 255.

Step 2 - Draw a Circle

Add the following line to your code:

  • Line 8 – Draws a circle during each frame of animation.
  • Then press Play to see your circle.

					function draw() {

Step 6 - Calculate acceleration towards the mouse

Add the following lines:

  • Line 23 – Create a vector to represent mouse position.
  • Line 24 - Recalculate the acceleration by doing vector subtraction between the mouse position and the circle’s position.
  • Line 25 – Scale the acceleration to a sensible value by forcing its size.

Press play and then move the mouse into the grey box. You should find the circle attempts to move towards the mouse. Likely it will ‘orbit’ the mouse.

It may be difficult to keep the circle on the screen, we will fix that in the next step.

Step 7 - Simulate Friction

Add the following lines:

  • Line 28 – Multiply the velocity by a factor during each animation frame. Make that factor less than 1 and that will create a ‘fake’ version of friction.

Press play and move your mouse around; you should find it much easier to keep the circle on the screen.

The velocity is being artificially reduced each frame, which simulates a force acting in the opposite direction to the movement of the ball.
