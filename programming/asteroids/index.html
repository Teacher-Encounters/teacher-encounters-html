Describe the Game

Wikipedia Article on the game

In asteroids, the player controls a ship. This ship is floating through space and the player views it as if from the top down. There are two forms of control for maneouvering the ship:

  • Rotating clockwise or counter clockwise using the left/right arrow keys
  • Thrusters to go forward and backward, using the up and down arrow keys
  • The final control is the ability to shoot lasers, although these have no effect on the movement

The player controls the ship in the vacuum of space, and they are surrounded by asteriods which are freely floating in space. The asteroids obey simple newtonian physics, and for this version of the game, if asteroids drift off the screen, they wrap around and appear on the other side of the screen.

The player will fire lasers at the asteroids and when they hit, the asteroid will break into multiple smaller chunks. Between 2 and 4 chunks, the size of which will be based on the size of the original and the number of chunks. Once an asteroid is small enough, when it is hit it will simply be vapourised.

All objects in the game will be under the influence of physics, asteroids will continue moving in one direction until they hit other asteroids, at which point they will bounce. When an asteroid is broken apart its pieces will fly in X evenly distributed directions. The players ship will continue floating in one direction until they use their thrust to change direction. The lasers will move in a single direction until they leave the screen, or hit an asteroid.

The player will score for each asteroid destroyed, smaller asteroids are worth more (harder to hit!)

The score will be shown in the corner of the screen

When the player collides with an asteroid, they are dead, game will read Game Over and they press 'fire' to restart

Now that we've identified our initial design for classes, let's think about these two characteristics for each class.

  • Associated data - will form the basis of attributes
  • Behaviour - which will form the basis of methods

Physics Entity

Attributes

To model simple newtonian physics, we will simply observe that:

Force -> Acceleration -> Velocity -> Position

We want these objects to move (change position), they do this because they have a velocity, the velocity can change due to acceleration, and the acceleration is caused by forces

Since this is a 2D game, each of these can be represented by a Vector

Methods

Physics objects will need to update their internal state each frame, so a method update() will tell them to use their own internal state to do this

To apply a force, we should probably have a method applyForce(force: Vector) which is then used on update() to modify the acceleration. For each frame of the animation, the force will be set to zero and then cumulatively added to as the overal simulation compels the objects to interact.
