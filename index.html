Teacher Encounters / Fusion

Teacher Encounters/Fusion connects teachers with software engineers and IT architects for clear, practical support on AS and A Level computing topics.

It provides real-world insight, guidance, and help with classroom exercises so teachers can link curriculum content to how skills and knowledge are actually used in industry.

The resources on this site have been written in collaboration with computer science teachers and software engineering industry professionals.

Teachers can use this site content to:

  • Enrich their curriculum teaching
  • Help students build a deeper understanding of how skills are applied
  • Help students see the essential skills needed for different roles
  • This knowledge can empower educators to effectively support students to take their best next step.

IT in the real world

The understanding, design, development, delivery and management of computer based systems in real world environments is rarely as simple as many text book examples can lead you to believe.

On this site we will describe the roles that exist to utilise the types of skills learnt in the AS and A level syllabus and how they are applied as part of various team structures.

  • Hierarchical Team Structure (Traditional)
